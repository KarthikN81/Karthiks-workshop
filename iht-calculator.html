Estimates your estate's Inheritance Tax liability under current HMRC thresholds, with allowances for children, pensions and gifting.

How this works: This tool estimates Inheritance Tax (IHT) using the 2026/27 nil-rate band (£325,000), residence nil-rate band (up to £175,000), and the £2m taper threshold. It's a planning estimate, not a substitute for advice from a solicitor or tax adviser — actual probate calculations involve case-specific reliefs HMRC will assess individually.

Assumptions in this estimate
  • Full NRB (£325,000) and RNRB (£175,000) available to a single person; married/widowed status can unlock up to double via spousal transfer.
  • RNRB tapers by £1 for every £2 the gross estate exceeds £2,000,000, and is lost entirely at £2,350,000 (single) or up to £2,700,000 (with full transfer).
  • Pensions are only included in the estate for deaths on or after 6 April 2027, per Finance Act 2026.
  • This is an estimate only — trusts, foreign assets, prior PETs, and specific reliefs may change the real liability. Speak to a solicitor or tax adviser for a formal calculation.
